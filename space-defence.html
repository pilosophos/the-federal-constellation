Spacecraft types

Spacecraft are colloquially divided into "ships" and "stations," although the line between them is not always clear. Stations may have small thrusters to correct its orbit, but are are not permanently equipped with superluminal drives. Subluminal ships are not equipped with superluminal drives, but are designed to move frequently. Superluminal ships are permanently equipped with superluminal drives.

Heavy weapons craft (HWC)

Heavy weapons craft are extremely large spacecraft outfitted with a variety of weapon types. HWCs that permanently defend a stationary target are known as weapon platforms. HWC ships are typically employed in symmetric encounters with large engagements.

Light weapons craft (LWC)

Light weapons craft are small, manoeuvrable combat craft used for asymmetric search-and-destroy tasks. They are commonly seen in anti-piracy and anti-guerrilla engagements. They are the most common type of weapons craft in the Constellation.

Shuttle

Shuttles are small, subluminal ships which transport small groups or individuals to and from planetary atmospheres. Superluminal shuttles are rarely seen, as warp drives are expensive and maintaining warp geometry is energy intensive.

Command and control craft

Command and control craft coordinate fleet movements and calculate weapon volleys. They are outfitted with a variety of sensors, as well as a compute cluster to analyze data from onboard instruments and from ships in its command. They host communications arrays and often comm-repeater probes to extend the range of their communications.

Recovery ship

Recovery ships allow other ships to dock with it to perform repairs and refuel in the field. They are equippable with modular cargo bays to store fuel and parts. They are important in deep-space combat where the nearest spacedock is far away. Recovery ships are distinguished from spacedocks by their ease of mobility, and do not build new ships.

Hospital ship

Hospital ships are mobile field hospitals. They are important in humanitarian missions, or if many ships are expected to operate away from a hospital for extended periods of time.

Engine ship

Formations of multiple engine ships are often used to create a large warp bubble around ships to transport them at superluminal speeds en masse. Each engine ship projects one part of the warp geometry at the same time as all the other engine ships, so transporting large areas requires more engine ships.

Although Constellation superluminal drives are also usable as weapons, engine ships differ from weapons craft in that it is specialized to carry an extremely large amount of fuel.

Engine ships are especially economical when transporting large fleets, since each ship activating their superluminal drives individually (if they have one at all) would require more resources. A few engine ships are usually held in reserve in case a primary engine ship fails. Engine ships can also be used as interdictors by warping spacetime in the opposite phase to the target, but not all interdictors are designed to be engine ships.

Interdictor

Interdictors are any spacecraft that prevent other ships from travelling at superluminal speeds. They are mainly used for protecting solar systems from warping spacetime, which would upset the motion of objects due to gravitational forces. They are also used in chase scenarios, to slow down and apprehend targets. Multi-interdictor formations are common to extend the range or effectiveness of interdiction.

Communications craft

Communications craft are used to repeat and route superluminal communications to extend the effective communication range. These can be either mobile or stationary. Stationary communications craft are usually a part of some permanent infrastructure like the Constellation Internetwork, whereas mobile communications craft are used in deep space either to maintain a connection with the nearest base or to create an isolated ad-hoc network.

Munitions

Multi-role Plasma Drive (MPD)

Multi-role Plasma Drive (MPD)

TypePlasma drive
Use3 mutually exclusive roles: Superluminal drive, Interdictor, Shield/Weapon
Pros
  • Suited for many roles
  • Weapons fire doesn't require lowering shields
Cons
  • Must leave FTL to use as interdictor or shield/weapon
  • Must be provided with continuous power

Mechanism

The MPD contains a plasma generation system which vents plasma that can be manipulated by an array of electromagnets along the ship's hull into various geometries. This creates an electromagnetic shell around the plasma whose shape is calculated and controlled by an onboard computer.

A strange matter cyclotron is only involved when the MPD is actively used as a superluminal drive or interdictor, which injects strange matter into the plasma to satisfy the negative energy requirement for warping spacetime. The strange matter decays too quickly to be meaningful in battle, and continuous strange matter generation is too resource-intensive to be sustained.

Usage

As a shield/weapon

For the shield/weapon role, the ship leaves superluminal speed and the strange matter cyclotron is switched off to give more power to the plasma generator and electromagnets. The electromagnetic field by confines incoming plasma projectiles within the EM shell, thereby acting as a shield. For kinetic projectiles, the plasma contained in the EM shell is hot enough to melt or vaporize most materials. The electromagnets can also eject an EM shell which contains plasma, to send projectiles outward as a weapon.

As a superluminal drive

For the superluminal drive role, the strange matter cyclotron is switched on, which satisfies the negative energy requirement for warping spacetime. The result is a plasma geometry that contracts spacetime in front of the ship and expands spacetime behind it, moving the space between the warped regions at superluminal speeds. The most common plasma geometry for this is the soliton formation which generates self-propagating soliton waves.

As an interdictor

The interdictor operates by bending space in the opposite direction to another ship which is attempting to escape. Since the two spacetime bends are of opposite phase, they cancel out (like noise cancelling headphones) and create a locally flat spacetime. Since this role involves bending space, the strange matter cyclotron is needed for this role.

Limitations

The superluminal drive, interdictor, and shield/weapon are mutually exclusive because the differing plasma geometries required for each role are incompatible. However, switching between these three roles can happen at will, so for battle the standard operating procedure is to travel to the target at superluminal speed, slow the target using the interdictor, then engage the target at subliminal speeds. Another limiting factor is that each component requires continuous power to be effective. Even the plasma needs to be regenerated, since it radiates its energy in the form of heat and light.

Variants

An MPD without a strange matter cyclotron is known as a Fewer-role Plasma Drive (FPD) and cannot be used as a sueprluminal drive or interdictor. Multiple FPD units sometimes share an electromagnet array with the main MPD in order to provide extra firepower or defence in the shield/weapon role. Subluminal ships and stations also make use of FPDs, since the cyclotron is not necessary for subluminal travel.

Laser array friction system (LAFS)

Laser array friction system

TypeDirected energy weapon (radiation friction laser)
UseAnti-plasma interceptor, interdictor
Pros
  • Instantaneous burst
  • High precision even at long range
Cons
  • Slow recharge
  • Energy intensive

Mechanism

An array of high-energy, circularly polarized lasers comprising a single LAFS unit (colloquially known as a "cannon") is directed at a target and fired in an instantaneous burst. In a vacuum, the beam is invisible except at the target, since the photons all travel in one direction and scatter on contact with the target. This generates a strong magnetic field at the target through an Inverse Faraday effect driven by radiation friction.

Usage

Used to scatter electromagnetic energy in all directions, such as charged plasmas from weapons systems and warp drives. The scattering is useful in defending against weapons using electromagnetic fields to concentrate energy on a target. Scattering is also useful as a superluminal travel interdiction mechanism. Since Constellation warp drives operate by concentrating energy density in charged plasmas that are shaped through magnetic fields, the magnetic fields generated by the LAFS interferes with the magnetic energy "shell" which holds the charged plasma together, preventing the energy densities required to warp spacetime.

Limitations

Each laser burst requires a significant amount of electrical energy to fire. Normally, high-voltage capacitors are used to build up this energy, and rate-of-fire depends on the recharge rate of these capacitors. Most military spacecraft with appreciable armour plating are not greatly affected by a single burst, making the LAFS a primarily defensive weapon against energy projectiles.
